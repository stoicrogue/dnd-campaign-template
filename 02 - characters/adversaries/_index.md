# Adversaries

Villain narrative lore pages and their linked stat blocks. Adversaries are antagonists with significant story roles — not generic monsters.

## File Structure

Each major villain gets two files:
- `Villain Name.md` — narrative lore page
- `Villain Name - Stat Block.md` — combat statistics (or multiple if they have phases/evolutions)

Link stat blocks from the narrative page under a `## Stat Block` section.

## Narrative Lore Page Format

```markdown
# [Villain Name]

- **Role:** Primary antagonist / Secondary villain / Recurring threat
- **Affiliation:** [[Faction]]
- **Location:** [[Lair or Base]]
- **CR:** [Challenge Rating]
- **Status:** Active / Defeated / Dead

## Origins

*Where do they come from? What made them this way?*

## Goals

*What do they want? What are they working toward?*

## Methods

*How do they pursue their goals? What makes them dangerous?*

## Personality

*How do they interact with the world and the party?*

## Relationship to the Party

*History with the PCs, grudges, respect, manipulation.*

## Reveal & Roleplay Notes

*DM notes on how/when to reveal secrets, how to play them at the table.*
See [[05 - planning/Villain Name - Reveal FAQ]] for detailed guidance.

## Stat Block

See [[Villain Name - Stat Block]].
```

## DM Reference Files

For complex villains, consider a separate file in `05 - planning/` for:
- Reveal FAQ (what happens when the party learns the truth)
- Roleplay guide (how to voice and portray them)
- Encounter table (multiple possible combat scenarios)
